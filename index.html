The Kukuzetu Journey

The Diraja Vision

Diraja was founded in 2022 with a clear mission: Identify promising business opportunities in Africa, formalize them, and build the systems, transparency, and technology required to make them investor-grade. Many businesses in Africa operate informally despite strong demand and profitability. Diraja’s role is to transform such opportunities into structured, scalable ventures that investors can confidently participate in. Diraja focuses on ventures that:

• Address essential consumer needs

• Operate in large and growing markets

• Benefit from operational systems and technology

• Have clear paths to scale

Several ventures are currently being explored and developed within Diraja. Diraja operates as a venture studio - identifying opportunities, building the systems needed for scale, and then inviting investors to participate once a venture has demonstrated proof of concept.

The First Venture: Kukuzetu

Among the opportunities evaluated by Diraja, protein retail and distribution emerged as one of the most compelling.

In Kenya, demand for chicken and eggs is strong, yet distribution remains fragmented and inefficient. Consumers often face:

• Inconsistent quality

• High prices

• Unreliable supply

Kukuzetu was launched as a test venture to validate whether a modern, system-driven approach to protein retail could succeed.

2023 — Proof of Concept

Kukuzetu launched its first 2–3 retail locations in Nairobi. The goal was simple: • provide affordable protein • ensure consistent quality • deliver conveniently to customers Early results confirmed strong demand and repeat customer behavior.

2024 — Expansion and Learning

As demand grew, Kukuzetu expanded to multiple neighborhoods across Nairobi. This phase provided critical operational insights into: • supply chain management • inventory control • staffing and training • retail economics By the end of the expansion phase, Kukuzetu had grown to 12 locations serving hundreds of customers daily. We exited some unprofitable branches and learnt what needs to make a branch profitable.

2025 — Building the System & External Investor Interest

Rather than expanding rapidly, Diraja chose to strengthen Kukuzetu’s operational foundation. Key improvements included: • development of a proprietary retail management app • improved financial controls and reporting • standardized shop operating procedures • improved stock tracking and accountability These systems significantly improved operational transparency and shop-level performance. External Investor Interest During this period, Kukuzetu began attracting attention from investors. In June 2025, venture firm Kuzana approached Diraja with interest in investing in Kukuzetu. However, because the business was still in a phase of building systems, testing operations, and learning from expansion, Diraja chose to defer external investment and focus on strengthening the operational foundation first. This decision allowed the company to enter the next growth phase with stronger systems, clearer unit economics, and greater transparency for investors.

2026 — Scaling What Works

Kukuzetu has now demonstrated clear proof of concept. The venture has: • a growing customer base • validated shop-level economics • operational systems designed for scale For this reason, Diraja has chosen Kukuzetu as the first venture to be opened to external investors.



Why Kukuzetu First

While Diraja continues to incubate multiple ventures, Kukuzetu stands out because:

• Demand for protein is large and growing

• The retail model is simple and repeatable

• The supply chain can be vertically integrated

• Technology enables strong operational control

Kukuzetu represents the first validated venture emerging from the Diraja venture studio. Future ventures developed within Diraja will follow the same model — build first, prove the concept, then invite investors.

2024 — External Investor Interest

As Kukuzetu continued to grow, the business began attracting interest from investors. In April 2024, venture capital firm Kuzana approached Diraja with an offer to invest in Kukuzetu. At the time, however, the company was still in an important phase of building systems, testing operations, and learning from expansion. Rather than raising capital prematurely, Diraja chose to defer external investment and focus on strengthening the operational foundation of the business.



Why We Waited

The decision to defer funding allowed Kukuzetu to:

• Develop its proprietary operations management app

• Strengthen financial controls

• Improve shop-level accountability

• Validate unit economics across multiple locations

This approach ensured that the business would be more stable and transparent before bringing in investors.

2026 — Ready for the Next Phase

With stronger systems and clearer operating metrics now in place, Kukuzetu is entering its next phase: disciplined growth supported by strategic investment. For this round, we are prioritizing participation from:

1️⃣ Family

2️⃣ Friends

3️⃣ High-net-worth individuals

Institutional investors may participate in later stages once expansion accelerates.

2025 Operational Reset

Rapid expansion in early 2025 exposed operational weaknesses:
• aggressive price reductions
• increased distribution volume
• stock control weaknesses

In May 2025, management implemented a full operational reset:
• strict daily reconciliation
• leadership restructuring
• branch-level profit tracking
• accountability systems

Results:
• reduced stock leakage
• improved margin control
• restored profitability
Kukuzetu now scales from operational discipline rather than experimentation.
